Politics and Citizenship Draft

The Federal Republic of Worlds governs occupied space; Council of 11 holds veto; jump-tax & licensing; sharp citizen-vs-resident legal divide.

Overview

The Federal Republic of Worlds governs all occupied space and everything between member worlds. A Senate and Commons handle most governance, but a Council of 11 worlds (7 founding, 4 added) holds veto power. Member worlds self-govern within federal law; violators are blockaded, then face military intervention in the extreme.

Details

  • Commerce & licensing: the jump tax tolls gate use per route; licenses authorize space operations. Who holds which license, and where, is the quiet currency of frontier power.
  • Citizenship is everything. Citizens are federally protected, can appeal against member worlds, move freely, and cannot be executed by a member world (only the Military, only for treason). Earned by birth or service; revocable.
  • Residents (non-citizens) have none of this — deportable, travel-bannable, at the mercy of local law. On a frontier vice-town like Thides Station, this means a population of people who can vanish and be ruled to have “skipped town.”

Cross-Domain Links

  • FTL and Hyperspace — gates, jump tax, and the licensing that lets corps operate in the dark.

World Rules

  • Citizens get uniform federal protection, federal-law supremacy, right to appeal against member worlds, and freedom of movement. A member world cannot execute a citizen; only the Military can, only for treason (else deport/deny entry). Citizenship is by birth or by military/government service; revocable.
  • Non-citizens (residents) abroad have no federal protection. Violations → deported to home world + travel-banned, plus local punishment. The home world must accept them back.
  • PCs are citizens by default; playing a non-citizen requires the Second-Class Citizen disadvantage.
  • Inter-world commerce is the Republic's main lever: ships pay a jump tax to use gates (per-route rate), and the Republic licenses people/corps for space operations, including in unoccupied space.
