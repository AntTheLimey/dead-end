The mechanical scaffold and hooks are here — name, history, and voice are Jay’s to write.

Shack is a frontier surveyor who got good enough at finding things to think he could get rich at it. His edge is real: he invented a better way to scout the unknown — a nanotech recon rig that oozes around corners, down cracks, and into dead ships and relays back what’s there before he ever sticks his neck out. It made him briefly valuable and then it made him reckless. One failed mission later he owes X Bank more than a lucky strike could cover, so he did the frontier thing: he skipped the system and took the only job at the far end of nowhere. He is Curious, Overconfident, and too trusting to leave a mystery alone, and he’s chasing the one great discovery that clears his name — exactly the kind of loose end the dead system makes of people.

AUDIT — for Jay & Tim

Player-to-player notes, not a GM ruling. All rules costs RAG-verified (book/page cited).

  1. Gadgeteer [25] → Gizmo ×2 [10] + Signature Gear [2]. In a limited super-science TL11 game, raw Gadgeteer is close to a trap: Standard Gadgeteer inventions take real downtime, need an Engineer skill (the old sheet didn’t have one), and depend on Tim green-lighting invention. Gizmo is the trait Jay was actually reaching for — “pull out the device that fits the situation,” instant, no support skill (B57). Signature Gear then anchors his one iconic invention so it can’t be taken. Net: same “gadget guy” identity, cheaper, faster at the table, 13 points freed for the explorer.
  2. DX 14 → 11 (−40 pts), reinvested. A DX-14 build pays 80 pts for gun/fist reflexes a surveyor barely uses. Those points went into Per +2, Sensors 18, the tech cluster, and survival advantages. He’s now the best in the room at finding things, which is the concept.
  3. Weapon redundancy gone. Beam+Guns+Gunner (all at DX+0) collapsed to one Beam Weapons (Rifle) at a better level (13). Reclaimed ~4 pts.
  4. Gadgeteer needed Engineer — now it’s there for the right reason. Engineer (Electronics) represents that he designed the recon rig; it’s backstory-made-mechanical, not a Gadgeteer tax.
  5. Broad-and-shallow → concentrated. The old ~30 skills at 1 pt each became a deep signature cluster + a focused exploration/ship spread. Fewer skills, better numbers, clearer character.
  • Signature Gear on the recon rig — a quick nod. It’s cheap (2 pts) and thematically perfect: the bank stripped him of everything except the invention that’s still his.
  • X Bank Enemy + Debt — needs a name and a face. Suggestion below; happy to tie it to a lender with reach into the Thides System so the debt follows him to the dead end (very on-theme).
  • Heavy self-control load (Curious/Overconfidence/Gullibility 12, plus Obsession & Unluckiness): at SC 12 he gives in roughly 1-in-4 times, so these fire often. That’s Jay’s established taste — just confirming it’s the intensity he wants.
  • Starting wealth — the loadout below is man-portable and modest; confirm the wealth level and how Debt interacts with starting cash at your table.

A BLURB FOR TIM — X Bank (skeleton hook, Jay to voice)

[!info] Draft hook — Jay finalizes in his own voice; Tim owns the canon The failed mission: Shack fronted a survey/salvage run on borrowed money — his recon rig said there was a payday out there. There wasn’t, or someone got there first, or it cost more to reach than it was worth. The lender wants its principal back with interest he can’t cover.

Why they’re after him: he didn’t default quietly — he skipped. Town, then planet, then system. To a lender with frontier reach, a runner is a message: chase him or every borrower runs. So a collections/repo team is still on his file — not many, and not close, but they don’t close files.

The hook for Tim: make the creditor a bank whose paper reaches the Thides System, and the debt follows Shack to the dead end — a lever you can pull whenever the plot wants pressure. He took the MacMillian job partly because it’s far enough out to stay ahead of them.

Name options (pick/replace): Ardent Mutual · Corvid Financial · Halloran Trust. (Avoids collision with existing vault entities — sniff before finalizing.)
