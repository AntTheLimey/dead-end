Concept

A genuinely brilliant trauma surgeon — the doctor you wanted in the room on the worst night of your life, who has since picked up some rough combat skills out of sheer work conditions. Then it was his worst night: his own child on the table, a detail missed under pressure, and a death he spent and then borrowed past everything trying to prevent. The child died anyway. Struck off, bankrupt, and hunted by the people he owes, he ran to the dead-end of the charted lanes — the one place that doesn’t check a license or a ledger. Now he keeps strangers alive with uncanny skill and cannot refuse anyone who is hurt, because he failed the one who mattered.

Rock Lavey carries himself like a man holding very still so nothing spills. Steady hands, a flat clinical calm that only cracks when someone young is bleeding — then something behind the eyes goes very far away for a half-second before the training takes back over. He watches everyone’s breathing without meaning to. He answers to “Lavey,” “Rock,” or nothing at all, and visibly dislikes “Doctor.”

He was the surgeon other surgeons called. Then his own child got sick — or hurt — and he was the one holding the chart. Under pressure, exhausted, certain, he missed something. To fix it he spent his savings, then his practice, then borrowed far past anything he could repay. The child died anyway.

What came after took the rest: a negligence finding on planet Wu, a revoked license, creditors who don’t forgive, and a marriage that didn’t survive the empty chair. He ran — out past the gates and the tax lanes, to the frayed end of the charted dark, to the Thides System, where the only employer hires any warm body with no questions and nobody runs a records check on the new “security” hire. MacMillian is the bottom of the barrel, which is exactly why it’s the only door open to him. This is his last option.

A non-combatant who survives by defense and distance. Dodge 8, Brawling/Punch 10 (Parry 8, 1d-3 cr), basic competence with sidearms and beam pistols at skill 10 — broad but shallow, the “rough combat skills picked up from work conditions.” His real weapon is First Aid 17 / Diagnosis, Physician, Pharmacy, Physiology 15 / Surgery 14, all already including Healer 2 and boosted by Higher Purpose in a life-saving clutch. He keeps the crew alive; he does not win firefights.

Crew connection to Six: Lavey is the one person who scanned Six, learned what he is — and said nothing. The doctor who could have turned him in for the bounty and chose mercy instead, because mercy is the only thing he has left to give. Six owes him a wordless debt; Lavey owes Six nothing and helps anyway. The campaign’s two debtors, bound by a kept secret.

The “sharp eye.” Mechanically this is Intuition (the diagnostic hunch), Photographic Memory (perfect recall of faces/charts/doses), Sensitive + Detect Lies 13 and Body Language 13 (reads people), and Danger Sense — not a dedicated Observation/Search stack. He notices what’s wrong the way a clinician notices a wrong vital.

Two separate threats (don’t conflate them):

  • The debt (modeled by Dead Broke) — the money he borrowed and lost trying to save the child. The creditors are back home; they explain why he owns nothing, not who walks onto the station.
  • The charge (modeled by Secret) — a planetary negligent-homicide warrant from planet Wu. As a Republic citizen, the Thides Federal Marshal won’t extradite him on another world’s charge. But the bounty is portable, and the dead end is not as far away as he hoped: the ticking clock is word of it reaching Thides, after which freelance bounty hunters can come to take him in. Player-facing upside — it’s freelancers, not the law, so the crew can fight, bribe, hide, or outrun the threat.

Why he fits Dead End: he is the campaign’s thesis before the plot starts — a man who knows a truth (his own failure) he can never set right, who will recognize the station’s slow death because he already failed to stop one. The Vow-driven wiring guarantees he gets pulled into other people’s trouble, and the bounty clock is a personal subplot Tim can light whenever he wants the dead end to stop feeling safe.
