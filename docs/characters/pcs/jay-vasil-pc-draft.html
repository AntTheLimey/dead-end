Nothing below is invented backstory — it’s only what the traits point at. Name, history, and voice are Jay’s to write.

A sharp, restless frontier generalist wired to find things nobody else has — an inveterate tinkerer (Gadgeteer) with a prospector’s eye (Archaeology, Geology, Survival), the hands to fly the ship and work its sensors (Piloting, Navigation, Electronics Operation), and a head that does the math in real time (Lightning Calculator, Less Sleep). He is driven — to the point of Megalomania — by the conviction that he will make the great discovery out in unknown space, and he is Curious, Overconfident, and Gullible enough to walk straight into whatever that takes. He carries a Debt and an Enemy (“X Corp”) out of his past. In Dead End’s terms, he is a man built to not let a mystery lie — exactly the kind of loose end the dead system makes of people.

AUDIT — for Jay Vasil

Player-to-player notes, not a GM ruling. Ordered: must-fix (legality) → build effectiveness → setting ties. All rules costs verified against the GURPS RAG server (book/page cited).

1. You’re 3 points over the 175 cap (GCA “Unspent −3”). The build costs 178. Cleanest fix: take quirks. You currently have zero, and GURPS lets you take up to 5 quirks (−1 each) on top of the −75 disadvantage cap (B100). Three quirks → exactly 175; five quirks → 175 with +2 to spend. Quirks are also free characterisation — e.g. “never throws away a broken gadget,” “narrates his own plans out loud,” “won’t fly on the 13th” (leans into the Triskaidekaphobia). This is the recommended route since your disadvantages are already maxed at −75 — quirks are the only lever left to reclaim points.

2. Your Tech Level is set to TL3 — the campaign is TL11. Every /TL skill on the export prints /TL3: Beam Weapons/TL3, Vacc Suit/TL3, Spacer/TL3, Piloting/TL3, Electronics…/TL3. None of those things exist at TL3 (that’s crossbows-and-galleons tech — no beam weapons, spacecraft, vacc suits, or computers). It’s a GCA default that never got switched to 11. Fix: set the character’s TL to 11 and every /TL skill retags to /TL11. No point change — TL-tagged skills cost the same at any TL. This draft already shows the corrected tags.

3. Very broad, very shallow. ~30 skills, most at 1 point, a lot sitting at IQ−1/−2 or DX−1 — Diplomacy 12, Geology 12, Shiphandling 12, the Pilotings 13, the Navigations 13. That’s barely-competent across a huge spread. Consider concentrating: pick the 4–6 skills that are the character and raise them, and let the truly marginal ones go to default. Your deep skills right now are Archaeology 14, Computer Operation 16, and Survival 15 — good anchors.

4. Gadgeteer without the making skills. Gadgeteer [25] is your headline trait, but to invent or modify gear you roll against a building skill — Engineer, Machinist, Armoury, etc. You have Electronics Repair (Computers/Comms/Sensors), which covers electronic gadgets nicely, but there’s no Engineer (any). Adding Engineer (Electronics) — even at 1–2 points — is what turns Gadgeteer from “flavour” into “builds the thing that saves the session.” Strongly worth a couple of points if you trim elsewhere.

5. Weapon-skill redundancy — ~5 points you could tighten. You have Beam Weapons (Pistol + Rifle) and Guns (Pistol + Rifle) and Gunner (Beams), all at DX+0. In a TL11 energy-weapon setting, Beam Weapons is the one that matters; slug-thrower Guns may rarely come up. Dropping Guns to default (you still roll DX−4 = 10 in a pinch) reclaims 2 points toward Engineer or a quirk buy-back. Keep Gunner (Beams) if you expect to man ship/vehicle weapons.

6. Survival (Woodlands) is your deepest outdoor skill — but you’re a space prospector. 4 points in Woodlands is odd next to Geology (Rock Worlds). If Tim’s frontier runs to barren/rock/ice worlds, retarget those 4 points to a matching Survival specialty (Mountain, Desert, or a Space type) so the investment lands where you’ll actually be. (Survival specialties default to each other at −3, so it’s cheap to re-aim.)

7. Thin durability for the frontier. HT 11 / HP 11, no armor bought, Dodge 9. Not illegal — just fragile, and HT rolls (staying conscious, shrugging off disease/poison/vacuum exposure) are average. If you free up points from #3/#5, even HT 11→12 [+10] noticeably hardens survival. Failing that, the armor in the loadout below is doing the heavy lifting — wear it.

8. Heavy self-control load — know what you signed up for. Curious (12), Overconfidence (12), Gullibility (12), Megalomania, Unluckiness, Phobia (13). At self-control 12 you resist on a 12 or less — roughly a 1-in-4 chance to give in each time you’re tempted, so expect these to fire often, and Gullibility + Overconfidence + Megalomania together is a combo that walks whole crews into trouble. Totally legal and great drama — just make sure that’s the intensity you want to play. (To soften one, raise its number toward 15, but that reduces the points you get, and you’re already at the cap — so it’s a play-feel choice, not a free one.)

9. “X Corp” (your Enemy) and the Debt are perfect hooks — give them a face. “X Corp” is a placeholder. The corporate powers you’d know in play are MacMillian Mining and Extraction Corporation (the crew’s employer). Ask Tim whether your Enemy and/or your creditor can be tied to a corporation or crew that reaches the Thides System — a debt that follows you to the dead end is exactly the campaign’s flavour, and it hands Tim a ready lever.

10. Megalomania → the great discovery in unknown space is a strong fit for a frontier-mystery campaign like Dead End, and a gift of a plot handle: you’ve built a character who cannot leave a mystery alone and will chase the first real anomaly the moment it appears. Worth flagging to Tim so he can aim it.

11. Confirm Gadgeteer fits the tone. Dead End is TL11 with limited super-science. Standard Gadgeteer (not Quick) is the realistic version — inventions take real time — so it likely fits, but it’s worth a one-line check with Tim that a gadgeteer-inventor is in-genre.

12. Name + one-paragraph bio. The sheet is “Character 1.” He needs a name, and enough of a past to explain the Debt and X Corp — happy to draft options with you once you’ve got a direction.
